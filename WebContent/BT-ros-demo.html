Relief rains down on Dalal Street Five reasons why Nifty reclaimed 7,800

The benchmark indices Sensex and Nifty were trading at over three-month highs in Wednesday's trade thanks to favourable macro-economic data, expectations of bumper monsoon and positive trend seen in global markets.

The 30-share index gained over 400 points, while 50-share index jumped past its crucial psychological level of 7,800.

Amid a spate of good news at home, International Monetary Fund (IMF) saying India will remain the fastest growing economy in the world was like the icing on the cake. IMF, in its World Economic Outlook on Tuesday, said India will grow at 7.5 per cent in 2016 and 2017.     

Market mavens are smiling on their fortunes, and below are the five reasons why:

1) Relief rains down on monsoon forecast

A day after country's private weather forecaster Skymet sounded optimistic on 2016 monsoon rains, India Meteorological Department (IMD), the country's official weatherman, also said monsoon would be "above normal" this year. The move sparked strong rally in farm sector stocks such as shares of fertiliser, irrigation systems and agri machinery manufacturers. Auto and FMCG stocks were also on the gaining spree.

2) IIP data positive for first time in four months

Industrial production grew at 2 per cent in February after remaining negative for three months. This bodes well for capital goods stocks such as shares of power, mining and consumer durables.

The only catch in the data is that the manufacturing sector, which constitutes over 75 per cent of the index, crawled at 0.7 per cent as against a growth of 5.1 per cent in February 2015.

3) Benign inflation raises rate-cut hopes

Retail inflation dropped to a 6-month low of 4.83 per cent in March, reflecting some improvement in the economy and offering further scope to RBI to stay accommodative. Reacting to the data, the S&P BSE Bankex index jumped nearly 2 per cent. This carries the highest weightage on the Sensex.

4) China's trade data rides Asia shares to 2016 highs

China's exports in March returned to growth for the first time in nine months, adding to further signs of stabilisation in the world's second-largest economy that cheered investors across the globe.

China reported exports jumped 11.5 per cent in March compared to a year earlier, the first increase since June and well above market forecasts. That was a huge improvement on February, even though data at this time of year tends to be distorted by the Lunar New Year holidays.

5) India shines bright, says IMF

At a time when IMF cut its global economic growth forecast for the fourth time in a year, it sounded positive on India. The IMF retained India's GDP forecast for this fiscal year and the next, confirming its status as the world's fastest-growing major economy.

It asked the government to cut down subsidies, initiate labour reforms and dismantle infrastructure bottlenecks to sustain strong growth.
